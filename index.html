Biography

I am a physician-scientist training in radiation oncology, with a clinical and research focus on primary central nervous system malignancies. My overarching career goal is to use modern and creative approaches to bridge the gap between benchside biological discoveries and bedside clinical care.

Currently completing my residency at MD Anderson Cancer Center, my laboratory experience interrogates the tumor microenvironment of glioblastoma using advanced spatial transcriptomics and computational imaging analysis.

Research Program

My research program is dedicated to understanding therapeutic resistance in neuro-oncology through a multi-omic lens. We leverage clinical data, tissue modeling, and computational methods to drive translational breakthroughs.

Spatial Tumor Microenvironment

Utilizing spatial transcriptomics to map the intricate interactions between immune infiltrates and tumor cells following ionizing radiation.

Radiogenomics

Correlating non-invasive MRI features with underlying genomic alterations to create predictive biomarkers for treatment response.
