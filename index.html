Bio

Hi! I'm Ugoma. I was born in raised in Florida where I attened both undergrad and medical school. I'm currently a resident physician training as a radiation oncologist with plans to specialize in primary central nervous system malignancies.

During my PhD training, I developed experience in molecular and cell biology, spatial genomics, RNA sequencing, computational analysis, and advanced microscopy applied to glioblastoma. This background informs my approach to studying tumor biology as an integrated, multi-scale system.

My overarching career goal is to use modern and creative approaches to translate benchside discoveries directly into clinically actionable insights that improve outcomes in cancer patients.

Research Program

My research program is dedicated to understanding therapeutic resistance in neuro-oncology through a multi-omic lens. We leverage clinical data, tissue modeling, and computational methods to drive translational breakthroughs.

Spatial Tumor Microenvironment

Utilizing spatial transcriptomics to map the intricate interactions between immune infiltrates and tumor cells following ionizing radiation.

Radiogenomics

Correlating non-invasive MRI features with underlying genomic alterations to create predictive biomarkers for treatment response.
