Bio

Research Overview

I established my research foundation during my doctoral training at The Scripps Research Institute in the Department of Molecular Medicine, in the laboratory of Dr. Michalina Janiszewska. There, I investigated the spatial organization of genetic heterogeneity and its relationship to cellular and structural features to better understand how the glioblastoma microenvironment contributes to tumor pathogenesis. Using multiplex imaging, spatial omics approaches, and computational image analysis, I characterized how distinct microenvironmental niches support tumor progression and therapeutic resistance in patient-derived glioblastoma specimens. This work integrated molecular biology, advanced microscopy, and bioinformatics to define glioblastoma as a complex, spatially organized system shaped by dynamic tumor–microenvironment interactions. During my graduate training, I also recognized the growing importance of machine learning and artificial intelligence in biomedical research. I expanded my computational skillset to incorporate these approaches into biologically relevant questions. In collaboration with investigators at Moffitt Cancer Center, we applied deep neural networks to model the spatial distribution of cellular populations within the tumor microenvironment. Additionally, in collaboration with the University of Florida, we leveraged large-scale imaging datasets to develop machine learning approaches linking morphological features to underlying mutational status. Together, this work strengthened my ability to integrate computational and experimental methods to interrogate tumor biology.

Key Focus: Spatial Omics • Multi-plex Imaging • Digital Pathology
