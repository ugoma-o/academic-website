Bio

Hi! I'm Ugoma. I'm a Florida-native, and have lived across the state throughout my training (north, south, and central FL!). I'm currently a radiation oncology resident physician with plans to specialize in primary central nervous system malignancies.

During my PhD training, I developed experience in molecular and cell biology, spatial genomics, RNA sequencing, computational analysis, and advanced microscopy applied to glioblastoma. This background informs my approach to studying tumor biology as an integrated, multi-scale system.

My overarching career goal is to use modern and creative approaches to translate benchside discoveries directly into clinically actionable insights that improve outcomes in cancer patients.

Research Program

My research program is dedicated to understanding therapeutic resistance in neuro-oncology through a multi-omic lens. We leverage clinical data, tissue modeling, and computational methods to drive translational breakthroughs.

Research Overview

My research integrates spatial biology, computational analysis, and clinical translation to understand how tumors evade therapeutic interventions. Combining next-generation sequencing, multiplex imaging, machine learning, and patient-derived models to identify actionable mechanisms of resistance in CNS malignancies.

Key Focus: Spatial transcriptomics • Cancer immunology • Radiogenomics • Translational oncology

Elucidation of Key Biological Mechanisms of Oncofetal Protein IMP2 in Pediatric High-Grade Glioma

Herbert Wertheim UF Scripps Institute | Post-Doctoral Research

Transcriptomic analysis and RNA sequencing pipeline development investigating IMP2 expression effects in pediatric high-grade glioma. Development of in silico comparative image analysis for tumor tissue identification.

Transcriptomics RNA-Seq Bioinformatics

Multiregional Biopsies and Neurosphere Cultures Reveal Spatial Divergence in Glioblastoma Evolution

Herbert Wertheim UF Scripps Institute | Graduate Research

Profiling of biopsies from MRI-defined tumor regions across surface, core, and deep margins. 5-ALA metabolite fluorescence separation for bulk and single-cell transcriptomics. Presented at SNO 2024.

Spatial Biology Multiplex Imaging Confocal Microscopy

Machine Learning Approach for Analyzing Texture and Morphometry Markers of Glioblastoma TERT Promoter Mutations

Past Research Projects

Collateral Lethality between HDAC1 and HDAC2 Exploiting Cancer-Specific NuRD Complex Vulnerabilities

Scripps Research, La Jolla CA | Graduate Research

Investigation of genetic interactions and synthetic lethality. Demonstrated HDAC2 inhibition suppresses growth of HDAC1-deficient neuroblastoma cells.

Published in Nature Structural & Molecular Biology (2023)

Single-Cell Heterogeneity of EGFR and CDK4 Co-Amplification and Immune Infiltration in Glioblastoma

Scripps Research, La Jolla CA | Graduate Research

Spatial profiling of oncogenic amplification and protein expression distribution. Identified patterns correlating with glioblastoma subtypes and immunosuppressive macrophage presence.

Published in Cell Reports (2023)

Spatial Analysis of Genetic Heterogeneity and Tumor Microenvironment Interactions in Glioblastoma

Scripps Research | Doctoral Thesis (Primary Investigator)

Investigated intratumoral heterogeneity and native spatial interactions between tumor and non-tumor cells. Developed platforms for single-cell in situ mutation screening.

Published in Journal of Clinical Investigation Insight (2024)

Methylation of TWIST1 Promoters Predicts Mesenchymal Stem Cell Function

Scripps Research | Graduate Research Assistant

Assigned MSC effector function phenotypes using TWIST1 methylation status. Developed potency assay and functionality scale for clinical applications.

Presented at FAU 10th Annual Medical Student and Resident Research Day (2019)

Research Collaborations

During my doctoral training, I became a member of the Florida Center for Brain Tumor Research (FCBTR) through my association with the University of Florida Preston A. Wells Jr. Center for Brain Tumor Therapy. The FCBTR is a multidisciplinary research collaborative, incorporating research institutions across Florida, focused on advancing the understanding and treatment of primary brain malignancies.

I conducted my thesis work in the laboratory of Michalina Janiszewska, PhD at Scripps Research. Throughout my training, I was fortunate to have worked closely with investigators, clinicians, and mentors from the FCBTR, Mayo Clinic Rochester, Moffitt Cancer Center, Dana-Farber Cancer Institute, and Cleveland Clinic Florida.
